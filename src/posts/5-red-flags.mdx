### 1. Vague or Overly Broad Language

If a policy says it collects "data you provide" or "information we deem necessary" without specifying what that data is, be wary.

- **Look for:** Specifics like "email address, name, IP address, location data."
- **Red Flag:** "We may collect personal information to improve our services."

### 2. Sharing Data with Unnamed "Third Parties" or "Partners"

Your data is valuable. A trustworthy company will be transparent about who they share it with.

- **Look for:** A list of partner categories (e.g., "payment processors," "advertising networks," "analytics providers").
- **Red Flag:** "We may share your data with our trusted third-party partners for business purposes."

### 3. Automatic Consent to Future Changes

Some policies include a clause stating that by continuing to use the service, you automatically agree to any future changes to the policy.

- **Look for:** A commitment to notify you of material changes via email or a prominent banner.
- **Red Flag:** "Your continued use of the service after changes have been posted constitutes your acceptance of the new terms."

### 4. The Company Claims Ownership of Your Content

For services where you upload content (photos, documents, art), this is critical. Many services grant themselves a broad, royalty-free, worldwide license to use your content.

- **Look for:** A clause that states you retain ownership of your intellectual property.
- **Red Flag:** "By uploading content, you grant us a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, modify, and distribute your content in any media."

### 5. No Clear Data Deletion Process

You have a right to be forgotten. A good policy will clearly explain how you can delete your account and what happens to your data afterward.

- **Look for:** A dedicated "Data Retention" or "Account Deletion" section with clear instructions.
- **Red Flag:** The absence of any mention of how to permanently delete your data.

By keeping an eye out for these red flags, you can make more informed decisions and better protect your digital privacy.
